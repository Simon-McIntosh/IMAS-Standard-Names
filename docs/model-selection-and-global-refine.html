§1 — Outcome and motivation

Two coupled deliverables. First, a complete role-by-role benchmark of the GPT-5.6 family (Sol, Terra, Luna) against the models currently holding each pipeline seat, so model selection is an evidence-based decision rather than a compose-only extrapolation. Second, a global-refine capability: a supported, budgeted way to run bounded quality campaigns over the accepted catalog (the immediate driver is the documentation-simplification backlog — ~1,250 accepted entries predating the strict-normative policy — plus the 129 accepted names carrying audit findings from the first full validation sweep). The two are sequenced deliberately: the refine campaign is the largest planned LLM spend, so the model seats it uses should be settled first — a cheaper, quality-equivalent refine model directly reduces the campaign's cost.

Guardrails locked by the lead: no naive full regeneration (cost-prohibitive and provenance-destructive); campaigns must ride the same generate→review→score pipeline as normal work (no privileged accept path); root causes are fixed in prompts/validators before any bulk pass so regeneration converges rather than churns.

§5 — Documentation-simplification campaign (pilot → scale)

Scope: ~1,250 accepted entries with pre-policy prose (typical values, estimator recipes, procedural padding) plus the 129 audit-flagged entries. Execution: 25-name stratified pilot first (all physics domains, mixed defect classes); lead reviews the pilot output directly; only then scaled batches (~100 names/batch) under the §4 gates. Cost model with measured per-doc numbers from §2 — at compose-bench rates the naive full-regen would be roughly $150–250, and the batched refine-only campaign with a Luna-class seat is projected well under half that, but the pilot supplies the real number before scale-up is approved. Done when: zero accepted entries carry banned-prose classes (machine-audited), audit-quarantine count on accepted names is zero or explicitly waived per name, and the expert-review-reply document's open item 1 is closed.

§6 — Risks

  • Refine churn: a below-par refine model rewrites correct physics. Mitigated by the convergence gate + pilot-first + review quorum unchanged.
  • Benchmark overfit: role benches on historical fixtures can flatter a model that memorised similar text; keep held-out samples and judge with the production rubric, not the training critique.
  • Cost surprise: 5.6 reasoning-token burn made list price misleading once already; every seat decision uses measured per-task cost.
  • Quarantine interactions: review pools skip quarantined names — campaign batches must re-validate after refinement or accepted fixes stay invisible.
