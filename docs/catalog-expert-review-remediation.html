§2 — Condensed expert review

  1. Documentation discipline: definitions are strong, but additional prose is often verbose, method-specific, speculative, or tangential; examples include thermal_plasma_energy and total_power_due_to_ion_cyclotron_heating.
  2. Provenance presentation: DD source paths should link to DD documentation and were absent in one reviewed release candidate.
  3. Grammar semantics: loci such as LCFS and ITB are usable tokens but have no first-class definitions discoverable in the catalog.
  4. Context preservation: generalising a DD path can erase the owning diagnostic component or local coordinate meaning, as for the X-ray crystal reflector’s x2_curvature.
  5. Coordinate ambiguity: “radial” denotes both cylindrical major-radius direction and a flux-surface-normal or flux label.
  6. State semantics: DD ion state may describe charge, vibrational level, or electron configuration; generated names and definitions over-specialised it to charge state.
  7. Name precision: several names omit plasma, wave-absorption, population, enclosure, or integration semantics, including ICRH power, thermal-electron power, bare power density, stored energy, and first-wall volume.
  8. Duplicate concepts: deposited electron power, plasma energy, and plasma current appear in overlapping bare/total/toroidal forms.
  9. Coordinate completeness: plasma-control and camera objects lack consistent cylindrical coordinate triplets; locus definitions are only indirectly visible through quantity documentation.
  10. Physics correctness: the safety-factor sign text differs from the DD convention, and the minimum-q locus should be based on minimum abs(q) if that is the intended DD concept.
  11. Domain taxonomy: lower-hybrid antenna pressure, computational-workflow gyrocenter quantities, and the broad mechanical-diagnostics bucket appear misplaced or reveal weak domain definitions.
  12. Editorial simplicity: definitions such as flux-loop effective area use needlessly complicated polarity language where a short positive/non-negative constraint would suffice.

§6 — Risks and sequencing constraints

  • Do not treat similar wording as proof of duplication; source scope, aggregation, integration domain, and physical mechanism must be compared first.

  • Changes to DD-derived semantics may need upstream DD resolution; quarantine rather than rationalise an authoritative inconsistency.
  • Release-site verification must use the rendered deployment artifact in addition to the graph, because source hyperlink failures are presentation/export defects.

§ Decisions

How should names distinguish cylindrical major-radius components from flux-surface-normal or flux-label quantities?

Define radial exclusively as the cylindrical R component in the right-handed (R, phi, Z) frame. Introduce flux_surface_normal for signed vector projections normal to magnetic flux surfaces, positive toward increasing flux label. Retain perpendicular for magnetic-field-relative semantics; it is not a synonym for flux-surface normal. Keep radial_coordinate_of_* as cylindrical major-radius coordinates and distinguish flux-coordinate derivatives from physical unit-normal projections.

Must component identity such as reflector be encoded in the standard name, or may structured source-context metadata carry it?

Live DD evidence: channel/reflector is an optional set of reflectors directing plasma light toward the crystal. X3 is the reflector-surface normal oriented toward the plasma; X1 is the local tangent direction chosen to be more horizontal than X2; X2 is the orthonormal local tangent direction uX2 = uX3 × uX1. Therefore X2 is not the machine vertical direction, and vertical_front_surface_radius_of_optical_element asserts unsupported semantics. The DD source inspires the quantity but does not dictate its canonical SN wording.

Use descriptive local-axis terminology rather than DD labels. Retain first and second local tangential directions because the DD defines a physical ordered frame: the third direction is normal to the object surface or plane and points toward the plasma; the first tangent is horizontal or more horizontal than the second and points in the positive toroidal direction; and the second tangent satisfies e2 = e3 × e1 . Encode the intrinsic object class and local tangent direction when they change quantity meaning; retain diagnostic, channel, and storage hierarchy only as structured provenance. Remove registered X1/X2 labels. Never reinterpret the second local tangent direction as global vertical or either tangent as a principal-curvature direction without evidence.

What is the primary rule for physics-domain assignment when source subsystem, diagnostic method, and measured physics disagree?

Assign the primary domain from the semantic subject and function of the standard name. Plasma quantities follow the physical phenomenon they describe; intrinsic plant or diagnostic component quantities follow the component's functional subsystem. Source IDS, producing workflow, and diagnostic method remain secondary facets and must not override the named quantity's meaning.

Should the minimum-safety-factor locus minimize signed q or abs(q) , and should resolution wait for an upstream DD clarification?

Define the minimum-q locus as arg min |q|. The associated q_min value is signed q evaluated at that locus, not the non-negative magnitude |q|. This preserves the DD q_like COCOS transformation: a q sign reversal leaves the locus unchanged and reverses the stored value. Poloidal flux at minimum safety factor therefore means flux at arg min |q|.

For accepted ambiguous names, should this programme favour immediate canonical renames or documentation-first correction followed by staged lineage migration?

For names that have not been accepted through a merged catalog PR, apply the canonical rename immediately through supported graph operations, atomically retarget semantic DD sources, and compact the superseded node into private StandardNameChange history after a reviewed dry-run manifest; do not publish a deprecated alias. For PR-approved names, use a staged successor and governed public deprecation through catalog PR review. RC presence does not confer approval, and an identifier is not reused while an uncompacted predecessor still occupies it.

When should a DD ion/state source become ion_charge_state rather than generic ion_state in a standard name?

The reviewed fast_ion_state_power is superseded and fast_ion_charge_state_power exists, but accepted ion_state families still often define state as ionization charge. DD ion/state is a generic resolved-state container that may include charge number, vibrational level, or electron configuration. The generator must use the leaf quantity and identifier context, not infer charge solely from the parent path.

Use ion_charge_state only when the DD leaf, identifier, or other explicit source evidence resolves ionization charge. Otherwise retain generic ion_state or name the actual internal-state axis such as vibrational level or electron configuration. Parent path spelling alone is not evidence.

How is catalog PR acceptance represented independently from LLM review acceptance?

The project is still in its first RC cycle and no standard name has yet been accepted through a catalog PR. RC presence and catalog_edit origin do not imply governance approval.

Retain accepted as the LLM name-review threshold and add approved as the subsequent lifecycle state set only by a merged catalog acceptance PR. RC export/import must never set approved. Persist PR number/URL, merge commit, and approved_at; superseded names are retained as governed deprecations only when superseded_from_stage was approved.

What documentation belongs in a rigorous standard-name definition?

The review identified diagnostic lists, estimator recipes, typical values and practical commentary as verbose or inaccurate.

Definitions state identity, equations, domain, sign and exclusions. Measurement or computation methods appear only when constitutive or required to distinguish the quantity; diagnostic inventories, estimator recipes, typical values, advice and padding are prohibited.

How should grammar concepts such as loci be defined and presented?

Loci such as last_closed_flux_surface and internal_transport_barrier were grammar tokens without first-class discoverable definitions.

Model governed compositional concepts as StandardTerms in ISN YAML, separate from complete quantity StandardNames. Define every locus; expose term list/fetch/search APIs and SPA detail surfaces. LCFS and ITB are display/search abbreviations only, never parser aliases.

How should DD sources and enhanced descriptions appear in the catalog?

The graph contains raw DD documentation and richer derived descriptions, while source links must resolve to the exact DD release used by the name.

Link to the exact pinned DD version and path, never latest. Show authoritative raw DD documentation with meaningful-parent fallback for terse leaves, then separately label enhanced context as derived/generated. Do not expose operational model, hash, cost, timestamp or edit-ledger fields.

How should source provenance and superseded unapproved names be retained?

DD sources were retained, but source ownership and edit chains were inconsistent across refine, regenerate, cascade and reconcile paths; operational lineage leaked through public outputs.

Retain immutable semantic DD-source snapshots and centrally retarget them through supported edits. Store operational transformations as private StandardNameChange history exposed only through an explicit trace tool. Compact unapproved superseded nodes into internal change events after a reviewed dry-run manifest; preserve governed deprecations only for PR-approved names.

no-cocos-in-descriptions

Standard-name descriptions must NEVER name a COCOS number. Sign and coordinate conventions are stated explicitly in physical terms (e.g. q positive when B_phi and I_p are parallel; right-handed cylindrical (R,phi,Z)). The DDv4 basis is COCOS 17 (DDv3 was COCOS 11) but neither number appears in prose. COCOS is carried only in a dedicated graph/catalog metadata field, not in the human-readable definition. A catalog-wide sweep must strip any existing COCOS mention and a regression guard must reject new ones.

domain-move-mechanism

Apply the ~30 confident physics_domain reclassifications via a reviewed direct Cypher SET (physics_domain + source_domains) on the live graph, then re-export. In the same campaign, locate and fix the code/LLM prompt that assigned the incorrect domains at root (dd_domain_classifier.py / definitions/physics/ids_domains.json / domain_gold_set.json) and add a regression unit test that guards the corrected assignments so future runs cannot reintroduce them.

gyrocenter-domain

Lead confirmed transport (2026-07-16): guiding-center orbit dynamics is kinetic/orbit-transport physics; the distributions IDS default is transport; fast_particles is too narrow (all charged particles have gyrocenters) and not an existing domain. Applies to gyrocenter_frequency, poloidal_gyrocenter_frequency, toroidal_gyrocenter_frequency (computational_workflow -> transport) and, for family coherence, gyrocenter_density + gyrocenter_current_density (null -> transport). The code override (path_domain_overrides.json) already encodes transport; its provisional marker can be retired.
